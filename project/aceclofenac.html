Aceclofenac is indicated for the relief of pain and inflammation in osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis and ankylosing spondylitis.

Key Facts:

1. Aceclofenac has been prescribed to relieve pain and inflammation.
2. Take this medicine with food or milk only to prevent stomach upset.
3. Take it according to the dosage prescribed by your doctor and correct the time duration.
4. Long-term use may cause some serious complications such as stomach bleeding and kidney problems.
5. It may cause dizziness, somnolence, or visual disturbances. Use caution while driving or doing anything that needs to be concentrated.
6. Avoid drinking alcohol while taking Aceclofenac because it can cause excessive sleepiness and increase your risk of stomach problems.

Side Effects:

Some of the common side effects of aceclofenac are:
  • Abdominal pain
  • Constipation
  • Diarrhea
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Skin rash
  • Dizziness
  • Visual Disturbance
