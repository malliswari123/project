Saridon is an analgesic commonly used for headaches, common cold, and menstrual cramps. It is an over-the-counter medication that provides instant relief to patients complaining of aches associated with fever, toothache, and arthritis-related aches.

Saridon is commonly used for the following ailments:

1. Headache & Sinusitics.
2. Fever associated weakness and muscle aches.
3. Pain caused by dysmenorrhoea and menstrual cramps.
4. Joint aches caused by arthritis.
5. Dental Pain.
6. Chronic Migraine.

What are the side effects of Saridon?

Some of the few common side-effects caused by Saridon are :
-> Skin rashes, allergic reactions to any NSAID can cause red rashes and itching.
-> Tachycardia or a faster heartbeat.
-> Changes in a mood like anxiousness, fear, restlessness, and nervousness.
-> Decrease in appetite and lack of sleep.
-> Saridon sometimes causes diarrhea, edema and elevates toxic levels in the liver.
-> Saridon causes abdominal pain when taken on an empty stomach or at wrong hours, or over-consumed.
